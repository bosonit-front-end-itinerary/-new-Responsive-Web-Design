Here's a time line of Rocio Jurado's life:

  • 1960 - Rocío Jurado won her first singing competition at the age of 16, in her hometown of Chipiona, Spain.
  • 1962 - She made her professional debut at the Teatro Calderón in Madrid, Spain.
  • 1965 - Rocío Jurado represented Spain in the Eurovision Song Contest with the song "¡Qué bueno, qué bueno!", finishing in the 13th place.
  • 1970s-1980s - She became one of the most popular and successful singers in Spain and Latin America, releasing numerous hit albums and singles.
  • 1984 - Rocío Jurado made her acting debut in the Spanish film "Marineros de agua dulce".
  • 1991 - She was awarded the Gold Medal for Fine Arts by the Spanish government.
  • 1995 - Rocío Jurado received a Latin Grammy Award for Best Folk Album for "Como las alas al viento".
  • 2001 - She was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer and underwent surgery to remove a tumor.
  • 2005 - Rocío Jurado gave her last public concert in the bullring of Chipiona, her hometown.
  • 2006 - She passed away in Madrid at the age of 61 due to pancreatic cancer. Her funeral was attended by thousands of fans and fellow artists, and she was buried in the cemetery of Chipiona, Spain. Her legacy as one of the most beloved and successful singers in Spanish music continues to this day.
